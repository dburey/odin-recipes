Festival

Description

Jamaican festival is a sweet, fried cornmeal dumpling that’s a beloved companion to jerk dishes and other savory island favorites. Made from a simple blend of flour, cornmeal, sugar, and baking powder, the dough is shaped into elongated, finger-like pieces before being fried to a golden hue. The result is a slightly crisp exterior giving way to a tender, subtly sweet interior, which beautifully complements the spicy, smoky flavors of jerk chicken or fish. It’s a comforting, must-try treat that captures the delightful fusion of textures and tastes in Jamaican cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups self-raising flour
  • 1 cup yellow cornmeal
  • ⅓ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp all-spice
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ cup cold water
  • vegetable oil for deep-frying

Steps

  1. In a large mixing bowl, sift in the self-raising flour, cornmeal, sugar and all-spice, mix until well combined.
  2. Add in the butter and incorporate until the consistency is grainy.
  3. In a cup, mix together the water and vanilla extract, begin adding it into the dry ingredients until the dough forms into a slightly sticky dough. Cover and let sit for 45 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into 8-10 equal parts. Flour a surface and roll each dough in a cylindrical fashion until you have two pointed ends. Repeat this process for all the dough portions.
  5. Heat up your oil to 350℉ in a deep large pot or pan, begin to drop in the festivals, it is fine to fry in 2 batches so the oil is not overcrowded. Let it fry on medium to low heat on all sides until golden brown and crisp.
  6. Transfer to a sheet lined with paper towels to drain off any excess grease.
