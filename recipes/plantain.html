Plantain

Description

Fried plantains are a beloved Jamaican side dish that highlight the natural sweetness of the ripe fruit. The plantains are peeled, sliced—often on a diagonal for a larger surface area—and then fried until they develop a beautifully caramelized, golden-brown exterior. Beneath that crisp shell lies a soft and sweet interior, offering a gentle counterpoint to the savory flavors of jerk meats, stews, or other traditional Jamaican dishes. Whether served as a tasty accompaniment or enjoyed on their own, fried plantains lend a comforting sweetness to the table that completes any island-inspired meal.

Ingredients

  • 3 Ripe plantain
  • ¾ cup Cooking oil
  • ⅜ tsp Salt optional

Steps

  1. Peel the plantain and slice into to pieces that are about ½ a inch thick.
  2. Add the oil to a frying pan and heat on medium to low fire.
  3. Add the plantain to the oil and fry for 3 to 5 minutes depending on how hot the old is. Always keep a eye o the plantain because they burn easily.
